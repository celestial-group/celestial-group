8:00-8:30

A sunny morning! John, a newly graduated civil engineer ,wakes up at 8 in the morning in a small apartment located in West Pymble. He has a busy day ahead of him. He needs to arrive at the CBD office by 9:00. After a quick breakfast, he goes straight to his driverless car and starts the engine...

8:30-8:32

Before getting on the Motorway, the car needs to navigate itself through several residential roads. Except in the designated driverless car zone, the residential roads are shared by driverless cars and normal vehicles. It means that John's fancy new car still needs to obey the current traffic rules like traffic lights and stop signs. Not the most fancy way to use a driverless car, but as more driverless zones gradually roll out, the situation will be much better.

8:32-8:38

The car is on the motorway! Before getting on the motorway, John has told the smart vehicle that the maximum toll he wants to pay is $5. The vehicle, bears that in its "mind", decides which lane to use to get John to the destination as quickly as possible while under budget. The price of each lane is diffrent. And it is based on the target lane speed and current congestion level. The prices are broadcasted to the vehicle's navigation system. And the vehicle can make its own decision about lane usages. John has nothing to worry on the motorway except that picturesque view that he can enjoy while the car is driving itself through the harbour bridge.

8:38-8:40

Finally the center of the metropolis. To maximum traffic flow, Sydney CBD is one of the first zones that have been designated as Driverless Zones. Only Driverless Vehicles are allowed. With the implementation of Autonomous Intersection Management System. The processing rate of the existing intersections have been doubled. The speed limit is also raised due to a reduction in the perception/reaction time of the vehicles. The result? Only two minutes for John to sprint from motorway exit to the office parking lot.

AIM System

Two lanes each direction, 55 km/h speed limit, Total Vehicles Processed in 150 sec: 190

Intersection Design

In order to make the AIM works, pedestrians cannot cross the roads. So pedestrian bridge and tunnel are created to help them get to the other side of the road.

Parking Lot

Because Driverless Zone marks the end of the Manually Driven Vehicles/Manually Driven Mode, a special Parking Lot is created for the drivers to switch the mode to fully automatic or park their cars and transit to autonomous public transport.
